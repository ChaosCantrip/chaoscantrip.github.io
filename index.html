James Donald

Hi! I'm James

I'm an Electrical Engineering student at Imperial College London. I started programming at a young age, and am now trying to find my way in a career as a software engineer. As they say, "If you love what you do, you'll never work a day in your life."

My Dad has the sole claim to responsibility when it comes to my love of programming. Back when I was 6, he took me to the Big Bang Fair, in Birmingham. At that event, there was a stall for Codecademy teaching basic HTML and CSS. That day, I went home, and burned my way through the entire web development course, and I fell in love with programming forever.

Shortly after that, he got me a Raspberry Pi for Christmas, along with a guidebook for Python, and I finally got my first taste of real code development. Since then, I've taken every opportunity to expand upon my programming skills through a multitude of personal projects.

My Biggest Project to date is SharkBot, a live-service Discord Bot that started off simple, but has now grown far beyond I could have imagined when I started, and has taught me more than I could recount about full-stack development, especially since the development of its' new accompanying website, SharkBot Online.

Please ignore the lorem ipsums, I'm still building this site :)

SharkBot

Python

SharkBot was my first large project.

Utilising Python, and the discord.py library, I built a bot to serve the Discord Server me and my friends use. Originally built to keep track of a #count-to-1000 channel we had going, it fast became my new hobby, adding more features and content as fast as we could come up with it.

Now, it has a plethora of features, from admin features to help us run the server to (free) lootboxes and an ever-expanding item collection game. More info can be found soon at SharkBot Online, though that website is still a work in progress.

Until recently, SharkBot was hosted by myself, running from a Raspberry Pi quite literally scotch taped to my bedroom wall. Unfortunately, I have moved house, and due to network reliability issues, this solution wasn't working as well so he now runs on an AWS EC2 Instance. Changes to the code are uploaded to GitHub, and the bot automatically fetches and applies them when needed.
